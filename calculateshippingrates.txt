To calculate shipping logistics rates, you need to consider several factors including the origin and destination, the weight and dimensions of the shipment, the type of freight (e.g., LTL, FTL, or parcel), and the chosen shipping service. You'll also need to factor in potential accessorial charges like liftgates or inside delivery. 
Here's a breakdown of the key elements and how to calculate shipping rates:
1. Determine the Type of Shipping:
Parcel shipping:
For small packages, use a parcel shipping calculator from providers like FedEx, UPS, or USPS. 
Freight shipping:
For larger shipments (LTL or FTL), use a freight shipping calculator from providers like FedEx Freight, UPS Freight, or TFE.
Container shipping:
For ocean freight, use a container shipping calculator from providers like Freightos or SeaRates. 
2. Gather Shipment Details:
Origin and Destination:
Provide the complete address or postal code for both the pickup and delivery locations. 
Weight and Dimensions:
Obtain the actual weight and dimensions (length, width, height) of your shipment. 
Freight Class:
For freight shipments, determine the appropriate freight class using a freight class tool (e.g., FedEx has a freight class tool). 
Package Type:
Indicate if it's a pallet, container, or individual package. 
3. Calculate Dimensional Weight:
If using dimensional weight (dim weight), calculate it using the formula: Length x Width x Height / 5000 (or another divisor, depending on the carrier). 
Compare the dim weight with the actual weight; the higher value is used for pricing. 
4. Factor in Accessorial Charges:
Consider any additional fees for services like liftgates, inside delivery, residential delivery, or handling of hazardous materials. 
5. Use a Shipping Calculator:
Input all the gathered information into a shipping calculator.
Compare rates from different carriers to find the best option. 
6. Consider Market Conditions:
Shipping costs can fluctuate based on market conditions, fuel prices, and available equipment. 
Consult resources like GoComet Freight Index or Freightos Baltic Index for market rate information. 
7. Explore Cost-Saving Opportunities:
Optimize packaging: Reduce the size and weight of your packaging to minimize dimensional weight. 
Consolidate shipments: Combine multiple shipments into one to potentially lower costs. 
Choose the right service: Compare express and economy options to find the best balance between speed and cost. 
By carefully considering these factors and utilizing online shipping calculators, you can accurately calculate and compare shipping logistics rates.
